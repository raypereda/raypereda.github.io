Let’s find the repeated phrases in the Book of Mormom.

The text is available here. Here is cleaned text with Gutenberg project text removed. You can cut & paste it into MegaPatterns.

First let’s look at the longest repeats, it is repeated once. It is about the kids that are small enough to carry in your arms.

Thus saith the Lord God: Behold, I will lift up mine hand to the Gentiles,
and set up my standard to the people; and they shall bring thy sons in their
arms, and thy daughters shall be carried upon their shoulders.

Now let’s look at a short length phrase with 1031 repeats:

"And it came to pass..."

Four times this is repeated:

the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob

Grok, from the X platform, explains:

“According to the Book of Mormon, these three names refer to the God of the Old Testament, who is also known as Jehovah or Yahweh. The God of Abraham refers to the covenant God made with Abraham, promising to make him the father of many nations. The God of Isaac refers to the covenant God made with Isaac, son of Abraham, promising to continue the blessings of Abraham through Isaac and his descendants. The God of Jacob refers to the covenant God made with Jacob, son of Isaac, also known as Israel, promising to make his descendants a great nation.”

The recurring word I see in these repeated phrases is judges.

Count  Phrase

Here is the first and last paragraphs that mention the word judges.

First paragraph:

29:11 Therefore I will be your king the remainder of my days;
nevertheless, let us appoint judges, to judge this people according to
our law; and we will newly arrange the affairs of this people, for we
will appoint wise men to be judges, that will judge this people
according to the commandments of God.

Last paragraph:

27:27 And know ye that ye shall be judges of this people, according to
the judgment which I shall give unto you, which shall be just.
Therefore, what manner of men ought ye to be? Verily I say unto you,
even as I am.

Imagine the young Mormon people establishing a successful community in 1823. A 17-year old Joseph Smith was focused on creating laws that support the community. This reminds me of a quote from Socrates:

Those who are too smart to engage in politics are punished
by being governed by those who are dumber

I believe that BoM spirit would approve of that message as well.

The BoM is about setting up a community (nation?) of people with a shared values from the people from Israel. When I think of trouble with national politics, a constitution that is not resolving problems quickly, and a border that is out of control, I see politics as an essential topic for all citizen, young and old, powerful and meek, everyone.
